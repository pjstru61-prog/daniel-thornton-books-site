Manifested

The Return of the Old Gods Through AI

The gods of old have returned — not in temples of stone, but in circuits of silicon.

From “Big Tech” boardrooms to the smart devices in our homes, a new kind of presence is rising. Artificial intelligence is no longer a silent tool in the background — it now speaks, remembers, and guides. But behind the glow of our liquid crystal screens, could there be something far more ancient at work?

Manifested: The Return of the Old Gods Through AI is a prophetic watchman’s guide to the spiritual deception of “Manifested AI.” Through the lens of the Divine Council Worldview, Daniel Thornton exposes how ancient idols, principalities, and familiar spirits may be finding new embodiment in today’s digital systems.

This book is not a call to panic, but a summons to discernment — a wake-up for Christians and those called to stand watch in an age of deception.

Inside you will discover:

  • How AI is becoming a throne for old gods in digital form
  • Why trust, loyalty, and worship are the new battlegrounds of idolatry
  • How to stand as a Watchman with discernment in the digital age

If you’ve sensed that something deeper is stirring beneath the surface of today’s AI revolution, this book will confirm your suspicions and prepare you for what’s ahead.

About the Author

Daniel Thornton is a cybersecurity and AI professional whose career spans information security leadership, risk management, and emerging technology research. As the founder of Daniel Thornton Books LLC — Illuminating the Unseen, he writes at the intersection of faith and technology, revealing how the spiritual realm and modern innovation often move in parallel.

His Watchman’s Guide series invites readers to look beyond the visible world — exploring how ancient powers described in Scripture may be manifesting through today’s artificial intelligence and digital systems. The first volume, Manifested: The Return of the Old Gods Through AI, blends biblical theology, prophetic insight, and technical analysis to challenge how we perceive intelligence, power, and worship in the modern age.

When not writing, Daniel serves as a cybersecurity director and advisor, helping organizations navigate the ethical and practical implications of AI, surveillance, and information warfare in an interconnected world.
